Sequenz 1
Warm-up Topics
Klick auf einen Topic → sieh dir die Fragen an → klick „Beispielantwort zeigen" und lerne, wie eine gute Antwort klingt.
🎬 Movies ▼
  • What kind of movies do you like? Why?
  • What do you use to watch movies?
  • Would you rather watch a movie at home or in a movie theatre?
"To be honest, I'm really into action and thriller movies – mainly because they keep me on the edge of my seat the whole time. As for where I watch them, I mostly use Netflix or YouTube. If I had to choose, I'd say I prefer watching at home – you can pause whenever you want and it's just more relaxed. That said, the cinema experience for big blockbusters is something else entirely."
Achte auf: Übergangswörter (To be honest, That said, As for), konkrete Begründungen, kein abruptes Ende.
✈️ Travel ▼
  • Do you like travelling? Why? Why not?
  • Where have you been so far?
  • Where would you like to travel to if you had the choice?
"Personally, I do enjoy travelling, although I haven't had the chance to go to many places yet. So far I've been to [Austria/Italy/...]. What I find fascinating about travelling is that you get to experience completely different cultures and ways of life. If I had the choice, I'd love to visit Japan – mainly because the mix of modern technology and traditional culture seems absolutely unique."
Hypothetische Frage (if I had the choice) immer mit Conditional II: „I would love to..."
💰 Money ▼
  • Where does your money come from?
  • What do you spend money on?
  • What would you do if you won the lottery?
"Well, most of my money comes from my apprenticeship salary. In terms of what I spend it on – mostly things like going out with friends, gaming, and occasionally clothes. As for the lottery question – I firmly believe the smartest move would be to invest a big part of it. But honestly, I wouldn't say no to travelling the world for a while either."
„In terms of..." und „As for..." sind starke Übergänge, die zeigen, dass du Struktur gibst.
⚽ Sport ▼
  • Do you do any sport? Tell us about it.
  • What kind of sport are you interested in watching?
  • Do you think that sport is good for a healthy life? Why?
"I do [play football / go to the gym] quite regularly. What I particularly enjoy about it is that it's a great way to switch off after work or school. When it comes to watching sport, I'm a big fan of [football / Formula 1]. And yes, I'm convinced that sport plays a huge role in a healthy lifestyle – not just physically, but mentally too. There's no doubt that regular exercise reduces stress significantly."
„There's no doubt that..." und „I'm convinced that..." klingen viel stärker als simples „I think".
📱 Mobile Phones ▼
  • What part does your mobile phone play in your life?
  • What do you think about banning phones at school?
  • If you had a child, how old should it be to have a mobile phone?
"Honestly, my phone is pretty central to my daily life – I use it for communication, music, navigation, pretty much everything. As far as banning phones at school goes, I can see both sides. On the one hand, phones can be a massive distraction. On the other hand, they're also incredibly useful tools for research. Personally, I'm of the opinion that it depends on how it's managed – a complete ban seems too extreme."
„On the one hand... on the other hand..." – perfekte Struktur für Diskussionsfragen.
🔧 Work ▼
  • What kind of work would you like to do in future?
  • Can you imagine working abroad for some time?
  • What do you think about working from home?
"Well, I'm currently doing my apprenticeship as an electrician, and I can definitely imagine specializing further in that field – maybe in renewable energy or smart home technology. Working abroad is something I'd genuinely consider – mainly because gaining international experience seems incredibly valuable these days. As for working from home – I think there's a lot to be said for the flexibility it offers, but personally I think a mix of both works best."
Dein Beruf (electrician) macht deine Antwort konkret und glaubwürdig – nutze das!
👥 Family & Friends ▼
  • What do you do in your free time?
  • How much time do you spend with your family? And with your friends?
  • What kind of activities do you do with your friends?
"In my free time, I mainly [game / hang out with friends / do sport]. When it comes to family – I'd say we're pretty close. We usually have dinner together on weekends and sometimes go on day trips. With my friends, we mostly [play football / go to the cinema / just hang out]. I think that balance between family time and time with friends is really important."
Konkret werden: Nenn echte Aktivitäten. Das klingt authentisch, nicht auswendig gelernt.
🍕 Food ▼
  • What kind of food do you like/not like?
  • What do you consider healthy eating?
  • What kind of restaurants do you like?
"I'm a big fan of Italian food – pasta and pizza especially. What I'm not so keen on is anything too spicy or sour. As for healthy eating – I'd say it's really about balance. You don't need to cut out everything you enjoy, but being mindful of what you eat regularly makes a big difference. Restaurant-wise, I enjoy [Italian / Asian / burger places] – places where the atmosphere is relaxed and the portions are decent."
„What I'm not so keen on..." klingt natürlicher als „I don't like..."
Sequenz 2
Monologue
Die Kunst: Struktur geben, eigene Meinung stark formulieren, Bilder interpretieren (nicht nur beschreiben).
Quote analysieren – Struktur
1
Paraphrase
Sag mit anderen Worten, was das Zitat bedeutet
"What this quote basically means is..."  /  "In other words, the author suggests that..."
2
Eigene Meinung – stark formulieren
Nicht: "I think". Besser:
"I firmly believe that..."  /  "I'm convinced that..."  /  "There cannot be any doubt that..."  /  "Personally, I hold the view that..."
3
Begründung + Beispiel
Belege deine Meinung
"Let me illustrate this with an example..."  /  "This becomes clear when you consider..."  /  "For instance, in my own experience..."
4
Gegenposition ansprechen (optional, aber stark)
"Of course, one could argue that... however..."  /  "Some people might say... but in my view..."
Meinung ausdrücken – Phrase-Upgrade
Schwach → Stark
"I think..." → "I firmly believe that..."
Schwach → Stark
"I don't agree..." → "I hold the view that... and I'm not alone in this."
Kommentieren
"It may sound obvious, but..."
"It is a truth universally acknowledged that..."
Erklären
"Let me be really clear about this:"
"What I mean by that is..."
Struktur
"First(ly)... Second(ly)... And finally..."
"There are a number of reasons, for example..."
Abschluss
"So, to wrap things up..."
"All things considered, I would say..."
Monologue starten – fertige Sätze
Start
"Hello and welcome to my talk about... My task is to..."
Struktur ankündigen
"I have structured my talk as follows: First I'll... Then I'll... And finally..."
Weiterleiten
"Now I'd like to draw your attention to..."
"Let me move on to my next point."
Auf Bild referenzieren
"Picture [X] has a clear message: it shows that..."
"The quotation given refers to the fact that..."
Sequenz 3
Dialogue Phrases
Diese Phrasen musst du flüssig abrufen können – sie zeigen, dass du die Aufgabe wirklich managen kannst.
Task-Linked Vocabulary – alle Kategorien
Starten / Aufgabe klären
"Now, let's see what we have to do."
"What would you suggest – how do we go about this?"
"Let's look at the steps we have to take."
Zustimmen
"That sounds good to me."
"Fine by me."
"You've hit the nail on the head."
"I couldn't agree more."
Höflich ablehnen
"I'm not really sure whether this would be helpful."
"I see your point, however..."
"I'm not really sure whether this makes any sense."
Off-topic / Sackgasse
"I think we're wandering off topic at the moment."
"I think we're going round in circles. That won't get us anywhere."
"Is this really a part of what we have to do?"
Weitermachen
"Right. Would you agree that we've completed this phase?"
"Let's see what's next."
"Shall we move on to [...], then?"
"Where do we go from here?"
Kompromiss finden
"Maybe we can agree that..."
"On the one hand... on the other hand..."
"Of course, you are right in saying that... but..."
"There is some common ground: we both agree..."
Partner einbeziehen
"What do you think about...?"
"Could you go along with that?"
"Is there anything you could add to this?"
Zeit kaufen
"That's an interesting question. Let me think for a moment..."
"Let me make this crystal clear."
"Now look here. Let me explain."
Häufige Fehler & wie du sie vermeidest
FehlerWas du stattdessen sagst
Lehrer alles reden lassen "What's your take on this?" / "Is there anything you could add?"
Thema verlassen "I think we're wandering off topic at the moment."
Schweigen / einfrieren "Let me think for a moment..." → dann Thema wechseln
Argument endlos wiederholen "I think we're going round in circles. Let's move on."
Diskussion „gewinnen" wollen Kompromisse suchen – das ist ein Bewertungskriterium!
Passiv sein, nur reagieren Selbst Ideen einbringen, Phasen aktiv abschließen
Prüfungsbeispiele
Mock Tasks
Klick auf „Beispielantwort zeigen" – lerne, wie gute Antworten klingen und welche Phrasen darin stecken.
Aufgabe 1 · Caffeine (Einzel)
Quotes in der Aufgabe
"Energy? What energy? Ha ha, the only energy I know is energy drinks. I'm totally like everyone else."
"What this quote basically means is that the speaker is completely dependent on energy drinks – to the point where they don't even know what natural energy feels like. I'm convinced that this reflects a very common problem in our generation: we rely on artificial stimulants instead of dealing with the root cause, which is often poor sleep or an unhealthy lifestyle. Let me illustrate this with an example: many students drink two or three energy drinks a day during exam season and then wonder why they can't sleep at night. Of course, one could argue that energy drinks are harmless in moderation – however, the quote suggests there's nothing moderate about this person's consumption."
Struktur: Paraphrase → Meinung (stark) → Beispiel → Gegenargument angesprochen
Dialogue Task

You and your partner have decided to limit caffeine. Collect alternatives to keep fit and awake.

herbal tea green tea cold water smoothies short walk power nap (20 min) fresh air stretching oats / nuts better sleep routine
Du: "Now, let's see what we have to do. We need to collect alternatives to caffeine that keep us awake. What would you suggest – how do we go about this?"

Lehrer: "Maybe we start with drinks?"

Du: "That sounds good to me. Herbal tea and green tea are obvious alternatives – they're much gentler on the body. Cold water also helps a lot, actually. What do you think about smoothies? They give you natural energy through the sugar in fruit."

Lehrer: "Good point. What about activities?"

Du: "Absolutely. I firmly believe that a short 10-minute walk does more for your focus than any energy drink. There's no doubt that getting fresh air resets your concentration. A power nap of 20 minutes is also scientifically proven to boost alertness. Shall we move on to food, then?"
Grün markierte Phrasen = Task-Vocabulary. Ziel: mindestens 4-5 davon natürlich einbauen.
Aufgabe 2 · Sports Tandem A – Football
Quote
"We are not a team because we work together. We are a team because we respect, trust and care for each other."
"What this quote basically means is that true teamwork goes far beyond simply doing a job together – it's built on mutual respect, trust and genuine care for one another. I hold the view that this is one of the most important lessons sport can teach us. There cannot be any doubt that a group of highly talented individuals will fail without trust, while a team of average players with strong bonds can achieve remarkable things. Let me give you an example: look at Leicester City winning the Premier League in 2016 – nobody gave them a chance, but their team spirit was extraordinary."
Konkretes Beispiel macht die Antwort unvergesslich. Nutze ein echtes Beispiel aus deinem Leben oder Allgemeinwissen.
Dialogue Task

You've decided to do more team sports. Discuss how + what diet could support your plan.

join a football club volleyball / basketball weekly schedule gym with a friend more protein less sugar meal prep hydration avoid processed food
Aufgabe 3 · Sports Tandem B – Volleyball
Quote
"Sports do not build character, they reveal it."
"What the author is essentially saying is that sport doesn't change who you are – it simply shows who you already are. Under pressure, your true character becomes visible. Personally, I hold the view that this is absolutely true. Think about it: when someone loses a game, do they shake hands gracefully or make excuses? That tells you everything. I'm convinced that sport is one of the best environments to observe human character – how people handle failure, pressure, and success says far more than anything else."
„Think about it:" ist ein starker rhetorischer Einstieg – weckt Aufmerksamkeit beim Prüfer.
Dialogue Task

Same as Tandem A – more team sports + diet plan.

Aufgabe 4 · Travelling (Einzel)
Quote
"Travel is the only thing you can buy that makes you richer."
"What this quote basically means is that unlike material possessions, travel enriches you in ways that money alone cannot – through experiences, perspectives and memories that stay with you forever. I firmly believe that this is true, although I'd add one nuance: you don't necessarily need to spend a lot of money to gain this richness. For instance, a weekend trip to a different city or region can open your eyes just as much as an expensive flight abroad. There's no doubt that experiencing different cultures fundamentally changes the way you see the world."
Nuance hinzufügen (‚although I'd add one nuance') zeigt kritisches Denken – das beeindruckt.
Dialogue Task

You and your partner plan a "Travelling on a Budget" day for your class.

country presentations typical food from each country travel quiz budget tips workshop cheap flights research hostel vs hotel comparison group tasks by region packing list challenge
